How to read the constructor table

Expected points are the risk-adjusted simulation mean. Pit points estimate constructor-only pit-stop scoring, while PPM measures expected points per $million. Open a team profile for both driver projections, qualifying and race components, the 90% interval and recent constructor form.

Why constructors change the best lineup

Each constructor combines two cars plus teamwork and pit-stop scoring, so the best pair cannot be chosen from driver rankings alone. A cheaper high-value constructor may free enough budget for a premium driver; a premium constructor can offer more raw points across both cars. F1 Fantasy constructor FAQ

Who is the top projected F1 Fantasy constructor right now?

For the current Belgian Grand Prix forecast, Ferrari ranks first at 59.5 expected points. Review price, value, risk and both drivers before choosing a pair.

How do constructors score in F1 Fantasy?

Constructors combine both listed drivers' qualifying and race scoring, qualifying teamwork bonuses and pit-stop points, with DNF exposure. Driver boost multipliers do not increase constructor totals.

Should I pick the highest-scoring or best-value constructor?

It depends on the whole lineup. Raw expected points favor premium teams, while PPM identifies budget efficiency. Compare complete legal teams because saving on one constructor can fund a stronger driver or second constructor.
